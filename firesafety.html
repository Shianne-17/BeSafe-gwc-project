Fire Safety

How to Prevent Fires

Knowing how to stop and prevent fires is especially important. You must stay calm when a fire breaks out to clearly assess the situation and take action. Remember to never leave food cooking unattended. Install smoke alarms on every level of your home (inside bedrooms) and test them every month. Keep all flammables 3 feet from anything that creates heat and if there is a fire, get out and call for help. Never go back inside for anything or anyone.

How to Put Out A Fire

Different fire extinguishers are used for different classes of fire.

Class A fires involve solid materials such as wood, paper or textiles.

Class B fires involve flammable liquids.

Class C fires involve gases.

Class D fires involve metal

Class E fires involve live electrical apparatus.

Class F fires involve cooking oils.

Always keep in mind that if there is a fire inside your house do not attempt to put it out, UNLESS it is a small fire and you have the correct fire extinguisher. The most recommended fire extinguisher for a household is an ABC fire extinguisher.
